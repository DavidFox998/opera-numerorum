\section{Response to Finding 2: Compact Support and R_tail}

\textbf{Supervisor response:} Correct. The document was imprecise. Let $p_5 = 3993746143633$ from certified M4. Then $2\log(p_5) = 58.03$.

For a Gaussian $F$, the tail is:
\[
R_{\text{tail}} = \sum_{\log q > 58.03} \log(q) F(\log q) \frac{2g}{\sqrt{q}}.
\]
Since $F(\log q) > 0$ for all $q$, $R_{\text{tail}} \neq 0$. The estimate $R_{\text{tail}} \sim 1.21\times 10^{17}$ is correct.

\textbf{Therefore:} A Gaussian centered at $\log(p_5)$ cannot be used. M9 requires a true compact-support test function.

With compact support, RHS(S_4) = $4.963 - 1818.33 + 2428.81 + 0 = +615.44$. Still fails.

With $S_5 = S_4 \cup \{p_5\}$ and support $[0, 2\log(p_5)]$, $R_{\text{tail}} = 0$ again. The archimedean term scales like $-C(S_5)$. For $C(S_5)=40.44$, RHS $<$ 0. This is what the script tests.
